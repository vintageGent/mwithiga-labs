Public Relations and Cybersecurity are often addressed as disparate silos. To the analyst, however, they represent a singular objective: **Ensuring the Integrity of the Signal.**

In an era governed by the **Data Protection Act (2019)**, institutional trust is no longer a purely communicative asset. It is a technical one. If an organization cannot secure the architecture through which it communicates, its strategic messaging is fundamentally compromised.

## Case Study: ThreatScopeV2 (Active Exposure Assessment)

A primary component of this practice is **ThreatScopeV2**. Rather than a traditional scanning tool, ThreatScopeV2 serves as an **Assurance Layer** for organizations seeking to understand their external digital footprint.

By prioritizing a modular, interactive architecture, the tool provides:
1. **Interactive Risk Assessment**: Guiding stakeholders through perimeter and web-application scans without the overhead of complex technical flags.
2. **Supply Chain Assurance**: Performing dependency audits against global vulnerability databases to ensure third-party code does not introduce institutional risk.
3. **Strategic Visibility**: Providing a "Lens" into the infrastructure that decision-makers can actually interpret and act upon.
